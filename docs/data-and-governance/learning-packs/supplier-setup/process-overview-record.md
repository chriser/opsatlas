## Overview

Supplier setup is the governed process for creating a new supplier, or changing an existing supplier, so the supplier can be used safely by downstream operational and finance processes.

The process starts with a formal business request. The requester prepares a supplier setup form and sends it to the support function. The support function checks completeness, coordinates any missing information and prepares the request for due diligence and credit checks.

Due diligence and credit checks are mandatory controls. If they fail, the setup request is paused or rejected and the requester is informed. If they pass, the supplier can move into operational master-data creation, finance-side setup and cross-system identifier mapping.

The supplier is not considered ready just because a supplier header exists. Readiness depends on controls, contract links, finance mapping, status handling and final confirmation back to the requester. The pack therefore treats supplier setup as a combined data, control and operational enablement process.

## Scope

In scope:

- New supplier setup.
- Changes to existing supplier details where the same controls apply.
- Request intake, completeness review, due diligence and credit checks.
- Operational supplier record creation.
- Finance-side supplier setup and identifier mapping.
- Contract/readiness controls.
- Activation and completion confirmation.

Out of scope:

- Real supplier onboarding decisions.
- Real finance master-data maintenance.
- Real contract negotiation or commercial approval.
- Confidential source reconstruction.

## Primary controls

- Formal request required before setup starts.
- Completeness review before onward processing.
- Due diligence and credit checks before progression.
- Failed checks stop progression.
- Supplier status must prevent premature downstream use.
- Operational and finance supplier identifiers must be mapped before payment-related processing.

## Open validation points

- Exact ownership of due diligence form completion.
- Exact sequence between operational setup, finance setup and contract completion.
- Final evidence repository for submitted forms and supporting documents.
- Detailed handling of failed due diligence evidence.
