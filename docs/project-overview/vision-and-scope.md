# Vision and Scope

## Vision

The AI Knowledge and Analytics Assistant is intended to help users understand complex business process knowledge through grounded, evidence-based answers. It will use controlled, anonymised or synthetic learning data rather than unrestricted confidential source material.

## Problem

Process knowledge is often fragmented across process maps, workshop notes, onboarding packs, operating model documents and subject matter experts. This can make onboarding, requirements clarification and transformation delivery slower and more dependent on informal knowledge routes.

## Proposed response

The proposed solution will use a Retrieval-Augmented Generation pattern. Source material will be registered, sanitised, prepared, indexed and retrieved before an AI model generates a response. The assistant should show evidence, support citations and avoid unsupported certainty.

## Academic positioning

DT602 is treated as the planning and ethical design phase. DT603 will become the execution/build phase. DT604 will provide retrospective evaluation against the plan and delivered outcome.

## Initial proof-of-concept scope

The first build phase will focus on:

- Controlled source register
- Synthetic/anonymised process learning data
- Basic ingestion and sectioning
- Searchable knowledge index
- Grounded Q&A response
- Basic validation and citation support
- Usage logging
- Test and evaluation evidence
- Azure DevOps governance trail

## Out of scope for the first proof of concept

- Use of confidential live organisational data outside approved controls
- Production deployment
- Direct replacement of accountable business roles
- Automated approval of business decisions
- Full enterprise access-control model
